As a COAPE companion animal behaviourist, I specialize in their behaviour therapy. In my work, I try to understand the animal, its emotions, motivation, and needs. In therapy, I build a relationship based on trust, cooperation, and communication between the animal and its caretaker. As a behaviourist, I do not accept aversive methods and violence. Therefore, during consultations, I observe the animal, conduct a thorough interview with the caretaker and work based on the MHERA model. I monitor the progress in behaviour therapy and adjust the working methods to the capabilities of the animal and its caretaker.

  • Advanced Diploma in the Practical Aspects of Companion Animal Behaviour and Training - granting British animal behaviourist qualifications - completed in 2022.
  • Specialist in animal therapy DipCABT
  • Member of the Association of COAPE Trainers and Behaviourists (CAPBT Poland)
  • Postgraduate Studies "Companion Animal Behaviour - Cats" at the Tischner European University in Krakow
  • 8+ years of experience in helping free-living and homeless animals in cooperation with a team of local volunteers
  • Master of Polish Philology, Adam Mickiewicz University in Poznań
  • Postgraduate Studies: Oligophrenopedagogy

My Services

Behavioural consultations for cats

Analysis of cat behaviour and development of an individual therapy plan to solve behavioural problems.

Support during the adoption process

Advice on choosing the right cat, preparing the home to welcome the pet, and assistance in the adaptation process.

Assistance in socialization and training of cats

Support in the cat socialization process, teaching proper behaviours, and cooperating with the owner in training.

Advice on optimizing space for cats (catification)

Analysis of living space and advice on arrangement and equipment of the home to meet the needs of the cat.

Collaboration with veterinarians to improve animal health and well-being

Cooperation with veterinarians to develop a treatment and care plan for the cat, taking into account behavioural aspects.
